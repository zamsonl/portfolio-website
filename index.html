Real-Time SOC Visualization Suite

A suite of live security dashboards built on Wazuh and OpenSearch, running on an on-premise Security Operations Centre. It turns raw firewall and endpoint telemetry into displays an analyst can actually act on: a wall-mounted global attack map, a drill-down analyst console, a live endpoint floor map, and an alert triage console.

  • Live telemetry pipeline Queries Wazuh alert and monitoring indices in real time through an OpenSearch proxy.
  • Canvas-rendered attack map Plots GeoIP-enriched firewall traffic on a custom world map with zoom, pan, and country drill-down.
  • Analyst triage workflow Severity and category filters, application-level drill-downs, CSV export, and audible critical alerting.
  • Built for a wall display Survives GPU context loss, auto-fits any screen, and runs unattended for days at a time.
Wazuh OpenSearch JavaScript Canvas GeoIP SIEM

04 — Background

About

I am a Year 2 Cybersecurity student at Asia Pacific University, and I learn best by building things that have to survive contact with real traffic.

What draws me to this field is the investigative side of it — understanding how an incident actually unfolded, how a SOC team decides what to chase first, and how evidence gets analysed clearly enough to stand up to scrutiny. Most of my time outside coursework goes into that: building dashboards that make noisy telemetry legible, working through forensic images, and turning what I find into documentation someone else could follow.

Right now I am extending my SOC visualisation work, writing up DFIR case studies, and looking for an internship where I can learn from an experienced blue team.
