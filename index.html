Running in production Flagship

Enterprise SOC Build-out

In August 2025 my employer was hit by ransomware. There was no central logging, so nobody could reconstruct what had happened or when. I was brought in to fix that, and spent the following months building the Security Operations Centre that now runs the company’s monitoring.

  • Wazuh SIEM on Ubuntu An all-in-one deployment — manager, indexer, and dashboard — tuned at the systemd level to stay stable under sustained ingest.
  • Six log sources, one pipeline Firewall, core switching, wireless access points, Windows endpoints, Active Directory, and a Zeek network sensor, normalised through custom ingest pipelines that resolve field-mapping conflicts between sources.
  • Detection content written for this network Custom multi-level decoders and correlation rules matched to the hardware actually in use, instead of generic rulesets that never fire.
  • Alerts that reach someone A bot pushes critical alerts to chat, so triage does not depend on somebody having the dashboard open at the time.
  • Hardened access path The dashboard gateway moved from an ad-hoc Python proxy to Nginx with TLS, subnet-level allow-listing, and host firewall hardening.
Wazuh OpenSearch Zeek Ubuntu Nginx Detection Engineering Incident Response

Configuration and detection content stay private — this is a live environment. I am happy to walk through the architecture and the trade-offs in an interview.

04 — Background

About

I am a Year 3 Cybersecurity student at Asia Pacific University, and I run security operations for Palmgold Management, a licensed gaming operator in Malaysia.

The ransomware incident in August 2025 is what shaped the work. Afterwards, nobody could answer the first question anyone asks: what happened, and when. There were no central logs to check. Everything I have built since comes back to that — getting the telemetry in, making it queryable, and putting it somewhere an analyst will actually look.

Most of it is less glamorous than the dashboards suggest. A fair amount of my time goes into tracing why a panel shows no data — back through the proxy, the index pattern, and the query window — until the pipeline is correct rather than just quiet.

Next I want to take the SOC from detection into response: automated blocking and host isolation through the firewall and switch APIs, and detection for ransomware behaviour such as canary files and mass renames. I am also looking for a team where I can learn from people who have run this at a larger scale.
